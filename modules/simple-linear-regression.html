Stage 04

The Penalty for Being Wrong

If we just add up the errors, positive and negative errors cancel out. To prevent this, we square the errors.

Squaring turns all negatives positive, and heavily punishes massive mistakes (an error of 10 explodes into 100).

Stage 07

The Model Scorecard

How much can we trust this line? We grade the model using your live data:

R² (Proportion of Variance Explained): 0.00
R² tells us what percentage of the variance in test scores is explained by study hours. 1.0 is perfect. We compare our line's errors (SSE) against the errors of just guessing the flat average (SST).

R² = 1 - (SSE / SST)

🧠 What SST actually is

SST = Total variation in your data

It measures: "How spread out are the actual scores from the average score?"

SST = Σ(y - ȳ)²

Translate that into English

  • y = actual value (like a student's score)
  • ȳ = average of all scores
  • y - ȳ = distance from the mean
  • square it → so negatives don't cancel positives
  • sum everything (Σ) → total spread
